Elmina

Elmina is a coastal town in Ghana's Central Region and the capital of the Komenda/Edina/Eguafo/Abirem District. It holds historical importance as the first European settlement in West Africa and is home to Elmina Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Once a major center for gold and slave trade, Elmina now relies on tourism and fishing as key parts of its economy.
